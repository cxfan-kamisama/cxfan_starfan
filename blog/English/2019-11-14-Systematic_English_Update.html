Systematic English Self-Study Plan Update

It has been one month since I carried out the Systematic English Self-Study Plan. However, only a few tasks are done in the past weeks. Daily email is the only task that has been done throughout the whole month. During the process, it has been found that while sometimes I was too busy to do the practice, I lost interests in some jobs, which should be improved. Therefore, it is time to update a new version of self-study plan.

Purpose

  • Develop good habits and consciously train my English proficiency.
  • Be able to speak confidently (not fluently since it is difficult to reach in the short term) in an informal setting.

Plan Details

Four components of English training and their corresponding plans are as followed:

  1. Listening
  • Daily Local News. Watch CBS news every evening during 7 – 8 pm without subtitles.
  • Morning Podcasts/Talks Listen to podcasts or watch TED talks alternatively every morning.
  • Purposed Listening Practice. Choose a story at npr.org with a transcript and plug the transcript into Vocab Grabber before listening. Look up some difficult words in the dictionary before listening to the audio without looking at the transcript. After listening to the audio twice, grab the transcript and read it aloud. Writing a summary of the story is recommended but not required for now. It is a good idea to share what you learned from some interesting stories this week in writing section or speaking section. This is required at least twice a week (Mon & Fri).
  1. Reading
  • Professional Reading. Two options are provided for each week: (1) finish one chapter of a book in your field and summarize the chapter using mind map and a short abstract; (2) read at least 3 articles in the current issue of the Science Magazine (Tue, Thurs & Sun). Before reading, copy and paste the material into Vocab Grabber, part of Visual Thesaurus to explore the words in it before reading. Write a short paragraph of summary in the daily email to friends (see the writing section below).
  • Leisure Reading. During the noontime, read some news articles, magazines, or book chapters as you like.
  1. Writing
  • Daily Email in a Group. Invite anyone who is willing to do this training into a group (with Pamela and Shaina). Write a daily email to the group to share the stories, the research progress and anything you want to with group members. Pay attention to the special instructions from other sections.
  • Monthly Essay. Given a topic, write a short essay to describe a thing, share a story or express your opinion every month.
  1. Speaking
  • Weekly Improvised Talk. Find a partner to practice English twice every week. (Wed & Sat)
  • Monthly Talk. Given a topic, deliver a short talk or presentation and record it.

The execution of the plans will be supervised by ELI 531 course lecturer Pamela. Weekly progress report should be delivered to the supervisor, no matter how you did last week. Further modification of the plans should be expected if the outcome is not satisfactory.
